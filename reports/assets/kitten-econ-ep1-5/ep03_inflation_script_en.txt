Kitten Economics Episode 3: Inflation

Welcome back to Kitten Economics.

In Kitty Town, fish tokens are used to buy snacks. At first, one token buys one fish treat. Then more tokens are printed while fish supply stays limited. Soon, one token buys only half a treat. That is inflation: prices rise because money grows faster than goods. Inflation quietly changes behavior. Families compare prices more carefully, delay non-essential purchases, and focus on value. To respond, track your weekly essentials and avoid emotional spending when prices jump.

Quick recap:
- Concept: Inflation
- Cat-world story: simple and visual
- Human takeaway: one practical decision rule
